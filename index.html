Part 1: What reflexive verbs mean.
Reflexive verbs are verbs we use when a person does an action to themselves. In English we sometimes say “myself,” “yourself,” or “herself.” In Spanish, we use small words before the verb: me, te, and se. These small words tell us who receives the action.

Part 2: Use me with yo.
Use me when you talk about yourself: Yo me levanto temprano. This means “I wake up early.” I am doing the action to myself.

Part 3: Use te with tú.
Use te when you talk to one person: Tú te bañas. This means “You take a shower.” You are doing the action to yourself.

Part 4: Use se with él, ella, or a name like Sonia.
Use se when you talk about he, she, or a person’s name: Sonia se prepara. This means “Sonia gets herself ready.” Sonia is doing the action to herself.

Exercise 6: Mini Story Practice

Spanish

Sonia se levanta temprano. Primero, se lava la cara. Luego, se cepilla el cabello. Después, se prepara para la escuela.

Su hermana también se levanta. Ella se viste rápido.

Por la mañana, Sonia se siente feliz. Pero cuando hace frío, Sonia se pone su abrigo.

English

Sonia wakes up early. First, she washes her face. Then, she brushes her hair. After that, she gets ready for school.

Her sister also wakes up. She gets dressed quickly.

In the morning, Sonia feels happy. But when it is cold, Sonia puts on her coat.
